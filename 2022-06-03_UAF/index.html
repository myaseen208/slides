Three Aspects of Design

Treatment Design

  • The treatment design determines the treatments to be included in the study: which treatments should we choose & how many?
  • The treatments may be determined by various treatment factors and level combinations of such factors.

Error-Control Design

  • Nature of Experimental Design to control and minimize Experimental Error

Sampling & Observation Design

  • Determines at which level observations are being taken and what kinds of observations are being taken

Quantitative Treatments

Quantitative Treatments

  • Four different amounts of nitrogen (N) fertilizer (0, 50, 100, 150 kg/ha)

Objectives

  • Quantify the dependency of Yield on amounts of nitrogen
  • Find the optimum amount of nitrogen for maximum Yield

Statistical Methods

  • Regression Analysis (Trend/Curve Analysis, or Response Surface Methodology)

Qualitative Treatments

Qualitative Treatments

  • Four Wheat Varieties (V1, V2, V3, V4)

Objectives

  • Comparing mean Yield for different Wheat Varieties

Statistical Methods

  • Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

Qualitative Structuredless Treatments

Qualitative Structuredless Treatments

  • Four Wheat Varieties (V1, V2, V3, V4)

Objectives

  • Comparing mean Yield for different Wheat Varieties
  • Comparing mean Yield for each pair of Wheat Varieties

Statistical Methods

  • Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)
  • Multiple Mean Comparisons (LSD, DMR, Tukey’s HSD, etc)

Qualitative Structured Treatments

Qualitative Structured Treatments

  • Four Wheat Varieties: MaxiPak (1960), Inqalab (1991), Galaxy (2013) and Ujala (2015)
  • Prior Information about grouping of treatments

Objectives

  • Comparing mean Yield for different Wheat Varieties
  • Comparing old and new group of Wheat Varieties
  • Comparing old Wheat Varieties
  • Comparing new Wheat Varieties

Statistical Methods

  • Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)
  • Contrast Analysis

Factorial Treatments

Factorial Treatments

  • Treatments are the combinations of different levels of Nitrogen (Low and High) and Phosphorus (Low and High)

Objectives

  • Comparing mean Yield for different treatment combinations
  • Comparing mean Yield for different levels of Nitrogen
  • Comparing mean Yield for different levels of Phosphorus
  • Comparing mean Yield for Nitrogen \(\times\) Phosphorus

Statistical Methods

  • Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)
  • Contrast Analysis

Basic Principles of Experimental Design

Basic Principles of Experimental Design

Randomization

  • Random allocation of treatments to EUs
  • It ensures validity of the estimate of experimental error
  • It leads to an unbiased estimate of variance as well as an unbiased estimate of treatment differences

Basic Principles of Experimental Design

Replication

  • Each treatment (or some of the treatments) must be applied to several experimental units
  • Replications will enable us to estimate the experimental (random) error against which differences among treatments are judged

Basic Principles of Experimental Design

Local Control or Blocking

  • The basic idea is to partition the total set of experimental units into subsets (blocks) that are as homogeneous as possible
  • The effects of nuisance factors which contribute systematic variation to the differences among experimental units can be eliminated
  • This in turn will lead to a more sensitive analysis

Selection of Superior & Stable Genotypes

Stability Analysis

Stability Analysis

  • Environment - Year, Location or combination of Year & Location
  • Multi-Environment Trials (METs) are conducted to evaluate plant genotypic performance & stability over a wide range of environmental conditions
  • Selection of the superior genotypes is fairly easy if their ranked performance remain the same over environments & consequently genotypes perform independently of environments
  • However, if the relative performance of genotypes varies across the environments then genotypes interact with environments

Selection of Superior & Stable Genotypes

Stability Analysis

  • The presence of significant genotype-by-environment interaction causes problems in selecting superior genotypes
  • Researchers have a long-standing interest in the development of objective techniques for selecting superior genotypes in crop performance trials
  • Statistical analysis of the data obtained from METs has received considerable attention, largely in response to the difficulties generated by significant genotype-by-environment interactions
  • Consequently, many methodologies have been proposed, developed and used for data analysis of the METs conducted by plant breeders
